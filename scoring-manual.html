  • Playing Handicap: Adjusted handicap based on course difficulty
  • Gross Total: Actual strokes taken
  • Net Total: Gross score minus handicap strokes
  • Stableford Points: Points earned (more is better)
Winners:

3. Entering Scores - NEW 3-Column Interface

3.1 Accessing Score Entry

Steps:

  1. Click on the SCORING tab
  2. Your group will be automatically selected
  3. You'll see all players in your group listed in a simple table

3.2 Understanding the NEW 3-Column Layout

What's Changed:

The scoring interface now shows 3 holes at a time instead of all 18, making it much simpler and easier to use:

  • Left Column (Blue): Previous hole
  • Middle Column (Green): Current hole being played
  • Right Column (Gray): Next hole

What You'll See:

The layout is organized as a simple table:

  • Players in rows: Each player gets their own row on the left
  • 3 holes as columns: Shows par, stroke index, and score input
  • Larger input fields: Easier to tap and enter scores on mobile
  • Color-coded columns: Blue (previous), Green (current), Gray (next)
Auto-Positioning: When you select your group, the system automatically positions to the last completed hole, so you're always at the right place!

3.3 Navigating Between Holes

Using the Navigation Controls:

At the top of the scoring table, you'll see navigation controls:

  • ‚Üê Previous Button: Move back one hole (shows one hole earlier)
    • Disabled at Hole 1
  • Viewing Hole Display: Shows which hole is currently centered (green column)
  • Next ‚Üí Button: Move forward one hole (shows one hole later)
    • Disabled at Hole 18
üí° Tip: You can navigate forward to enter scores for upcoming holes, or backward to correct earlier scores!

3.4 Understanding Handicap Stroke Indicators

Red Dots Show Strokes Received:

Above each score input box, you'll see red dots that indicate handicap strokes:

  • Each dot = 1 handicap stroke that player receives on that hole
  • Playing Handicap: Shown under each player's name (their slope-adjusted handicap)
  • Hover for details: Move your mouse over the dots to see "X stroke(s) received"
  • Automatic calculation: Strokes distributed based on hole difficulty (stroke index)
How it works:
  • Higher handicap players get more strokes
  • Harder holes (lower stroke index) receive strokes first
  • A player with playing handicap 20 gets 1 stroke on all holes, plus extras on the 2 hardest

Example - Reading the Player Info:

Amy
HCP: 18
Playing: 20

This means Amy's actual handicap is 18, but adjusted for the course slope it becomes 20. She'll receive handicap strokes as indicated by the red dots above each hole's score input.

3.5 Entering Scores for Each Hole

How to Enter Scores:

  1. Find the player's name in the left column of the table
  2. Note the red dots showing handicap strokes for that hole
  3. Click or tap on the score input box for the hole you want to enter
  4. Type the number of strokes taken
  5. The score saves automatically as you type
  6. Move to the next player or use navigation buttons to change holes
‚úì Auto-Save Feature: Scores save automatically - no save button needed!

3.6 Marking NR (No Return)

If a Player Doesn't Complete a Hole:

  1. Find the player and hole in the table
  2. Click the orange "NR" button below the score input
  3. The hole will be marked with an "NR" badge
  4. NR holes:
    • Will not count towards gross/net totals
    • Will show as "NR" in totals if any NR exists
    • Still calculate Stableford points (0 points for NR)
  5. To clear an NR: Click the √ó button on the orange NR badge
Example: If a player picks up their ball on hole 7, navigate to hole 7 and click the "NR" button for that player.

5. Understanding Handicap Calculations

5.1 How Handicaps Work

Playing Handicap:

The system automatically calculates each player's playing handicap based on:

  • Player's handicap index
  • Course slope rating
  • Course rating

Stroke Index (SI):

Each hole has a Stroke Index from 1-18:

  • SI 1: Hardest hole on the course
  • SI 18: Easiest hole on the course
  • Players receive handicap strokes on holes based on their playing handicap

Example:

If a player has a playing handicap of 12:

  • They receive 1 stroke on holes with SI 1-12
  • No strokes on holes with SI 13-18
  • This means their net score = gross score - 1 on those 12 holes

5.2 Stableford Scoring

Points System:

Stableford points are awarded based on net score relative to par:

Net Score Points
Double Eagle or better 5 points
Eagle (-2) 4 points
Birdie (-1) 3 points
Par (0) 2 points
Bogey (+1) 1 point
Double Bogey or worse (+2 or more) 0 points
Remember: These points are based on NET score (after handicap strokes are applied), not gross score.
